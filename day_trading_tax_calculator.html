Social Security & Medicare on W-2 wages: These are payroll taxes withheld automatically by your employer — you do not pay them via quarterly estimates. Capital gains from day trading are not subject to SS or Medicare. However, if your total income exceeds $200K (single) / $250K (MFJ), any unpaid Additional Medicare Tax (0.9%) shortfall must be paid via estimated tax (Form 8959). The NIIT (3.8%) already included above separately covers investment income for high earners.
Federal safe harbor: Pay the lesser of 90% of this year’s projected income tax or 100% of last year’s income tax (110% if prior AGI > $150,000). Pay at IRS Direct Pay (irs.gov/payments) or EFTPS.
